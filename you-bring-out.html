You Bring Out the Florida in Me

You bring out the child in me.
You bring out bubblegum pink, puckered coats of paint. The popstar in me. Katy Perry candy corset in me. Electric pink talons and aqua-lined lids.
You bring out the Annaleigh in me. The coily-haired girl with coffee mug eyes. My first sip of bittersweet friendship.
You bring out the gator in me. Callousness covered by turbid swamps.
You bring out the mystic. Mermaid-tail, winged, hooved goddess in me. Consumer of spirits, symbols, sorcery.
You bring out the Julia in me. Bushy brows hidden behind a shiny shield of black. Ally in the first strides of literacy. Co-traveler trampling across picture book pages.
You bring out the heiress in me. Princess of the tan Toyota. Lazing on a pink throne. Commanding my subjects, “turn up the music.”
You bring out the dread in me. Silently slithering through the stems of my conscience.
You bring out my wishes as frail as dragonfly wings. Pinned in the scrapbook of memory.
You bring out the clubhouse brat in me. Plaid skirt, collared shirt in me.
You bring out the southern Methodist sect in me. The “praise be” in me. The back pew heart attack in me.
You bring out the hope in me. Fingers crush chords of a major key.
